The Golden Pillar

Buy Gold Wisely

India is the world's second-largest gold consumer. Here's everything you need to know before making your next gold purchase.

India's Most Beloved Investment

Gold is woven into India's cultural fabric — from Akshaya Tritiya purchases to wedding jewellery to sovereign gold bonds. With over 25,000 tonnes held by Indian households, gold is both emotional and financial security.

Gold Necklaces
Temple jewellery, Mangalsutra, Rani Haar — crafted in 22K gold with intricate Indian designs
Gold Rings & Bangles
Everyday wearable wealth — rings, kadas, bangles in 18K to 22K purity
Gold Coins & Bars
24K investment-grade coins (1g–100g) and bars from MMTC, banks, and certified refineries
Digital Gold & SGBs
Sovereign Gold Bonds — earn 2.5% p.a. interest + capital gains, RBI-backed security
Gold ETFs
Stock exchange-traded units backed by physical gold — liquid, secure, and BIS-certified
Gold Earrings & Sets
Bridal sets, Jhumkas, Chandbalis — traditional & modern designs in 22K hallmarked gold

Smart Gold Buying Tips

Always check BIS Hallmark. Look for the 6-digit HUID code on every piece — it certifies purity and the jeweller's identity. Non-hallmarked jewellery may be under-karated.
Buy on auspicious days smartly. Akshaya Tritiya and Dhanteras drive up making charges by 15–25%. Consider buying 1–2 weeks before for the same gold at better pricing.
Understand making charges. Making charges range from 8–25% of gold value. Mass-produced machine-made jewellery has lower charges (8–12%) vs. handcrafted pieces (18–25%).
Track MCX Gold futures. MCX gold price is the Indian benchmark. Buying when MCX is at a discount to international prices (after INR adjustment) is ideal timing.
Exchange vs resell. Jewellers typically offer exchange at full gold value but not for making charges. Selling back yields 94–97% of current gold rate (minus making charges paid earlier).
